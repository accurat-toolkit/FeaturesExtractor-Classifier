Chechnya is a republic within the Russian Federation located in the Caucasus. Surface is 193 000 square kilometers, has a population of 1.103 million, and its capital is Grozny. It borders Stavropol Krai to the northwest, northeast and Dagestan to the east, south to Georgia and Ingushetia and North Ossetia to the west. After the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991, Chechen-Ingušetska ASSR is being divided into the Republic of Ingushetia and the Chechen Republic Ičkeriju declaring independence. After that came the first Chechen war with Russia, when Chechnya was owned de facto independence. After that came the second Chechen war, after which Russia took control of the territory. Since then, there are tendencies for the struggle for independence, there has been sporadic fighting in the mountains and the southern republics. 2006th The former president of Chechnya Alu Alkhanov has proposed changing the name of the republic in Nokčiin (Noxçiyn), which is called a transcription of the Chechen language.
