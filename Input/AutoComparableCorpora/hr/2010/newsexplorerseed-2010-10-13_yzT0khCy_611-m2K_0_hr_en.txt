Megabat was Megabazov son, a cousin of Darius the Great and the satraps Artaferna Elder, the Persian admiral [1] in the late 6th in 5th century BC. Kr. According to Greek historians, and later served as satrap of Phrygia [2] [3] [4], while the clay records of Persepolis mentioned on the site Megabata satrap Arahozije [5]. He is best known stories of Herodotus and his unsuccessful siege of Naxos 499th pr. Kr. led, together with millet ruler Aristagorom [6]. Megabata the leader of the expedition to the place appointed his cousin Artafern [1], a satrap, who ruled from the city lidijskog Sarda. According to Herodotus, Megabat because of quarrels with Aristagorom warned residents of the impending siege of Naxos [1], after which they have enough stock and endured four months of the Ionian-Persian siege [7]. It is assumed that the motive was to embarrass Megabatov Aristagoru the Persian royal court because of disagreements during the voyage to the island.
 Aristagorin failure against Naxos was the motive for raising the Ionian revolt [8], which in turn eventually led to the Greco-Persian Wars [9].
