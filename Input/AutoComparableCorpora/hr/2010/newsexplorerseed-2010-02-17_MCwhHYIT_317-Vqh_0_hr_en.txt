Intef II was an Egyptian pharaoh of the Eleventh dynasty during the First Intermediate Period. He was the capital of Thebes. At that time Egypt was divided between several local dynasties. After the death nomarch Ankhtifija, Intef has united the southern nomes until the First Cataract. After that it clashed with his main rivals, the nomarsima Herakleopolis Magne trying to gain control of Abydos. The city has several times passed from hand to hand, but Intef eventually prevailed, extending its authority in the north to the thirteenth nome. After these wars have been established better relations with its neighbors and the rest Intef reign was peaceful. Discovery Intef II statue, wrapped in festive robes sit up in the shelter Heqaib Elefanini suggests that the king&#39;s power extended to the area of the First Cataract, and perhaps even parts of Lower Nubia by 30 years of his reign. [1] This impression is confirmed by the expedition Djema Gebeleina started from the ground Wawat (Nubia) during his reign. [2] When Intef II died, he left behind a strong government in Thebes which controlled the entire Upper Egypt and had the border south of Asyut. [3] The earliest known dating of the god Amun at Karnak happened during his reign.
 Preserved section of the Turin canon mentioned Intef II, attributed to his reign of 49 years.
