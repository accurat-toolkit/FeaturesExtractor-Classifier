Merenre Nemtyemsaf I (2283rd pr. Kr. - 2278th pr. Kr.) Was the fourth king of the Sixth Dynasty. His nomen, teoforički Nemtyja calls, and once pronounced Antyemsaf, for what is now known to be incorrect. While the Merenrea Nemtyemsafa I once believed he was serving as current co-regent of his father, Pepi I Meryreu before I will reign, the publication of the Annals of the South Saqqara Stone that in 1995. made Vassil Dobrev and Michel Baud pokrazuje that Merenre directly inherited power from his father without any interregnum or a co-regency.
