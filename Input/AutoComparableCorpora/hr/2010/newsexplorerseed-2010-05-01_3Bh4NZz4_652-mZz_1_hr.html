JAVNI KONKURS ZA ZASNIVANJE RADNOG ODNOSA NA ODREĐENO VREME

CENTAR ZA ZAŠTITU I POMOĆ TRAŽIOCIMA AZILA/ASYLUM PROTECTION CENTER
11118 Beograd, Sime Igumanova 14

Objavljuje:
JAVNI KONKURS ZA ZASNIVANJE RADNOG ODNOSA NA ODREĐENO VREME


I Pozicija: psiholog na projektu borbe protiv trgovine ljudima (u trajanju od 1 godine sa mogućnošću produženja ugovora), jedan izvršilac.

II Opis poslova:
- rad na prevenciji trgovine ljudima kroz pružanje adekvatnih informacija kategorijama lica koje su izložene povećanom riziku od trgovine ljudima, a prvenstveno tražiocima azila na teritoriji Republike Srbije, što uključuje i realizovanje redovnih poseta odgovarajućim ustanovama u Beogradu ili u unutrašnjosti Srbije.
- obavljanje administrativno-tehničkih poslova kao i drugih poslova u skladu sa potrebama aktivnosti na projektu

III Uslovi:
1. VII/I stepen stručne spreme, diplomirani psiholog;
2. Poznavanje problema trgovine ljudima na medjunarodnom i nacionalnom nivou;
3. Prethodno iskustvo u radu sa ranjivim kategorijama, poželjno iskustvo u radu sa strancima;
4. Dodatna edukacija u radu sa ranjivim kategorijama, posebno decom;
5. Poznavanje engleskog jezika;
6. Poznavanje rada na racunaru (MS Office korisnički nivo)
7. Sposobnost efikasnog rešavanja problema i sistematičnost, organizovanost i pedantnost u radu;
8. Odlična pisana i usmena komunikacija;
9. Sposobnost utvrđivanja prioriteta i uspešnog obavljanja više zadataka istovremeno.

IV Kandidati koji dostavljaju prijavu na konkurs, dostavljaju fotokopiju diplome, svoju biografiju sa fotografijom u boji, motivaciono pismo, preporuke o ranijem iskustvu na sličnim projektima i edukacijama i izvod iz matične knjige rođenih.

V Prijave sa traženom dokumentacijom dostavljaju se Centru za zaštitu i pomoč tražiocima azila, ulica Sime Igumanova 14, Beograd, sa naznakom : „Za konkurs“ .

VI Rok za podnošenje prijave je 19. decembar 2010. godine.

VII Uz poštovanje svih prijava napominjemo da će biti kontaktirani samo kandidati koji uđu u uži izbor.

High Level of Police Achievements

At the very beginning of implementation of the new Asylum Law in April 2008, an Asylum Office was established as an organized unit of the Ministry of Interior of the Republic of Serbia, which issues first instance decisions on asylum seekers’ applications. Although this is something new, and although the asylum procedure in our country is still in its developing stage, and there are some technical problems, officers of the Ministry of Interior demonstrated while carrying out their tasks in this field a high level of commitment to cooperation not only with UNHCR but with the Asylum Center as well. Read More Namely, in most cases asylum seekers upon their arrival to our country first come into contact with the police, and then with the Asylum Office representatives. According to our experience, the police officers act properly towards asylum seekers so far; only a few irregularities were registered, which were corrected in a short time. The fact that police officers were able to notice irregularities very quickly, which were most often of technical nature and rarely the result of inexperience or lack of knowledge, confirms that the Serbian Police is fully aware of its role in the asylum procedure and determined to build-up legally perfect procedure, followed by maximal respect of human rights of asylum seekers from the moment they enter our country to the very end of the asylum procedure. New Regulations on Asylum Medical Examinations

Pursuant to Article 39 par. 2 of the Asylum Law of the Republic of Serbia, all asylum seekers soon after their arrival at the Asylum Center have to be medically examined according to rules and regulations to be given by the Minister of Health. Therefore, the Ministry of Health, i.e. the Minister of Health of the Republic of Serbia issued the Book of Regulations on Asylum Seekers Medical Examinations to be performed upon their reception in the Asylum Center (Official Gazette of RS, 93/2008, October 10, 2008; date of entry into force: October 23, 2008). The Regulations provide a legal framework governing medical examinations of asylum seekers when entering the Asylum Center, and list obligatory medical examinations to be carried out. Also, the content of the Form 1 is prescribed and it must be completed confirming in this way performed medical examination and state of health of the persons seeking asylum.

New RS Book Regulations on Asylum Application Form

The Minister for Interior of the Republic of Serbia signed the Book of Regulations on the content and form of the Asylum Application Form and personal documents that will be issued to asylum seekers and persons granted asylum or temporary protection (Official Gazette of RS, No 52/2008). This Book of Regulations defines the form and content of the Asylum Application Form according to Article 25 par. 4 of the Asylum Law, as well as the form and content of the ID card for asylum seekers according to the Article 58 par. 3 of the Civil Procedure Code. The Regulations defines the form and content of the Asylum Application Form, certificate for persons intending to apply for asylum, ID card for persons seeking asylum, ID card for persons granted asylum, and ID card for persons granted temporary protection (Article 1 of this Book of Regulations).

Asylum Law

National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia adopted long-expected Asylum Law on 24 October 2007. By this law, complemented by sub-law documents to be issued by the government, conditions will be provided for effective legal protection of asylum seekers on the territory of our Republic. Due to previous law provisions and lack of procedural rules, this was practically impossible to achieve. The new Law is in accordance with European standards, international obligations of Serbia and numerous international documents in the field of asylum law and human rights. The law will come into force on 1 April 2008.
